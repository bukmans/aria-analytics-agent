Project Overview

ARIA (Analytics Request Intelligence Agent) is an AI-powered, agentic web application developed as a capstone project for the AI for Business Leaders programme (Skillsbridges, 2026). It was designed and built to address a concrete operational challenge within the Advanced Analytics function of Catholic Relief Services (CRS) — one of the world's largest humanitarian organisations, operating across 70+ countries.

ARIA automates the intake, classification, routing, scoping, and ticket generation process for analytics requests submitted by CRS program and operations staff. It replaces a fully ad-hoc, manual process with an intelligent pipeline that operates around the clock, requiring zero human intervention from submission to triage.

Project Framing

ARIA sits at the intersection of three domains: Agentic AI (multi-step autonomous reasoning), Process Automation (Power Automate + email triggers), and Analytics Operations (demand management for a global analytics team). It is both a technical prototype and a strategic proof of concept for responsible AI adoption in a humanitarian context.

Problem Statement

The CRS Advanced Analytics team — comprising six specialists distributed across Luxembourg, USA, India, South Africa, and Honduras — serves a global organisation of thousands of staff across 70+ countries. The team handles a diverse and high-volume portfolio of analytics work spanning operational reporting, dashboard development, ad-hoc analysis, data pipeline engineering, and strategic insight generation.

The Core Problem

There is no formal process for submitting, tracking, or managing analytics requests. Requests arrive informally through email, Microsoft Teams messages, hallway conversations, and meeting follow-ups — with no standardisation of format, no classification system, no routing logic, and no tracking mechanism. This creates a cascade of downstream inefficiencies.

Root Cause

The absence of a structured intake mechanism means the analytics team operates reactively rather than strategically. Every request requires manual interpretation, clarification, and routing — a tax paid on every single piece of work, compounding across a six-person team managing global demand.

Organisational Stakes

In a humanitarian organisation like CRS, where analytics directly informs program decisions affecting millions of beneficiaries, capacity lost to process inefficiency is not just an operational cost — it has a real-world humanitarian impact. Analytics teams that cannot work efficiently produce fewer and slower insights for program delivery.

Proposed Solution

ARIA is a two-layer solution combining an AI intelligence layer (Claude API) with an automation layer (Power Automate) to create a seamless, zero-touch analytics intake pipeline.

Design Principles

ARIA was designed around four principles derived from CRS's Responsible AI commitments and the broader context of humanitarian data governance:

PrincipleApplication in ARIA
Human dignityRequesters are treated as intelligent stakeholders — ARIA generates clarifying questions rather than rejecting incomplete requests.
TransparencyEvery triage decision includes a rationale. Requesters receive a confirmation with their ticket ID, category, and assigned analyst.
Security by designThe API key is stored exclusively on the backend server — never exposed to the browser or end user.
Least interventionARIA automates only what is automatable without judgment. Final prioritisation and project acceptance remain human decisions.

What ARIA Does

When a CRS staff member submits an analytics request — either through the ARIA web portal or by emailing analytics-requests@crs.org — the system autonomously performs five sequential actions without any human involvement:

Step 1 · Classify
Request Classification
ARIA reads the plain-language description and classifies it into one of five categories: Operational Report, Dashboard Build, Ad-hoc Analysis, Data Pipeline, or Strategic Insight.
Step 2 · Score
Complexity Scoring
Each request is assessed for complexity (Low / Medium / High) and assigned a realistic effort estimate based on the scope, data sources likely involved, and category.
Step 3 · Route
Analyst Routing
ARIA routes the request to the most appropriate team member profile: Data Engineer, Data Analyst, Data Scientist, or Analytics Lead — based on the classification and complexity score.
Step 4 · Scope
Scoping Brief & Clarifying Questions
A professional 2–3 sentence scoping brief is generated, along with three targeted clarifying questions to send to the requester — dramatically reducing the back-and-forth that typically precedes work starting.
Step 5 · Ticket
Structured Ticket Generation
A complete project ticket is compiled with title, objective, data sources, expected output, success criteria, dependencies, and effort estimate — ready to be actioned by the assigned analyst immediately.

Two Entry Modes

ARIA supports two submission channels, designed for different user contexts:

Manual Portal — Chat-First

A four-step conversational interface: Intake → Analysis → Refine → Ticket. After submitting, ARIA immediately returns a preliminary classification and opens a focused chat to clarify scope. The requester answers questions, adds context, or asks back — then clicks Generate Final Ticket to lock in the structured output. This produces sharper tickets and fewer rework cycles than a one-shot form.

Email Automation (Primary)

Staff email analytics-requests@crs.org in plain language. Power Automate triggers instantly, calls the ARIA backend (/api/triage/email), and dispatches outputs to Teams, the assigned analyst, SharePoint, and the requester — with zero human involvement and a complete structured ticket returned in under 60 seconds.

Security Architecture Note

The Anthropic API key is stored exclusively as an environment variable on the backend server. It is never sent to the browser, never included in frontend code, and never transmitted in any URL or query string. The frontend auto-detects its environment: when opened via Live Server it contacts http://localhost:3000; when served by Express or deployed to Vercel it uses the same origin. For production, the backend can be deployed to Vercel with a single vercel --prod command using the included vercel.json — no restructuring required.

Backend

Node.js ≥18 Express.js dotenv cors express-rate-limit Vercel serverless compatible

A lightweight Express server acts as a secure proxy between the frontend and the Anthropic API. It validates inputs, holds three purpose-built system prompts (triage, chat, finalise), manages the ticket log, and enforces rate limiting (60 requests / 15 min). The API key is loaded from a .env file and never leaves the server process. A vercel.json file enables zero-config cloud deployment.

06

Development Process

ARIA was developed over a five-day sprint from initial ideation to a fully working, architecturally sound MVP. The process was iterative, with each build cycle surfacing new requirements and design questions that were resolved before proceeding.

Day 1 · Ideation
Problem Definition & Solution Selection
Eight project ideas were generated across workplace and non-workplace domains. ARIA (then called "Agentic Analytics Request Triage & Routing System") was selected based on speed-to-MVP, strategic relevance, and the availability of existing context from prior Decision Intelligence Agent work in Copilot Studio.
Day 2 · Proposal
Proposal Writing & Scope Lock
A one-paragraph capstone proposal was drafted covering problem, solution, mechanism, and impact. Platform selection was finalised: Claude API + single-file HTML frontend, chosen for speed, portability, and demo quality. ARIA v1 (manual portal only) was built and delivered.
Day 3 · Automation
Automation Layer Design & v2 Build
The distinction between AI assistance and true automation was identified as a critical gap. The full Power Automate pipeline was designed (12-step flow) and ARIA v2 was built with dual-mode interface (Manual Portal + Email Simulation), pipeline animation, and four simultaneous output destinations including requester confirmation.
Day 4 · Security
Backend Architecture & Security Hardening
API key exposure on the frontend was identified as a production-grade blocker. A Node.js/Express backend proxy was built to secure the API key in environment variables. The frontend was refactored to call the local backend exclusively, with a health check endpoint and auto-detection of backend status.
Day 5 · Documentation
Full Project Documentation
Complete project documentation produced covering all strategic and technical dimensions: problem statement, solution design, architecture, tech stack, development process, agentic design rationale, outcomes, success metrics, risk register, and future roadmap.
Iterative Quality Signal

Each build cycle surfaced a legitimate architectural concern — first the automation gap, then the requester notification gap, then the API key security gap. This iterative discovery and resolution process mirrors real-world responsible AI development practice, and demonstrates the value of progressive critique over big-bang design.

Agentic & Automation Design

What Makes ARIA Agentic

ARIA qualifies as an agentic AI system because it performs a chain of reasoning steps autonomously, producing different outputs at each step that feed into the next — without human intervention between steps.

Agent StepInputOutputReasoning Required
1. ParseRaw natural language requestStructured understanding of intentIntent recognition, context inference
2. ClassifyUnderstood intentRequest category (one of five)Domain knowledge, type discrimination
3. ScoreCategory + request detailsComplexity level + effort estimateScope assessment, experience-based estimation
4. RouteCategory + complexityOptimal analyst profileTeam capability matching
5. RefineInitial triage + requester repliesUpdated understanding across multiple turnsActive listening, gap identification, adaptive questioning
6. GenerateFull conversation history + session contextFinal scoping brief + structured ticketSynthesis, professional writing, structured output
Why Refinement Makes ARIA More Agentic

Step 5 (Refine) elevates ARIA from a single-pass AI tool to a true conversational agent. Rather than accepting one input and producing one output, ARIA maintains context across multiple turns — updating its classification, adjusting its effort estimate, and narrowing its questions as the conversation progresses. This iterative loop mirrors how an experienced analyst actually scopes a request, and it produces significantly higher-quality tickets as a result.

What Makes ARIA Automated

Automation is distinct from AI assistance. AI assistance requires a human to trigger the system and read the output. Automation means the system acts on behalf of humans without being prompted at each step.

Pipeline Performance

From email receipt to all four outputs dispatched: under 60 seconds. Zero human steps. Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, across all time zones in which CRS operates. This is the definition of automation — not AI that helps humans work, but AI that works instead of humans on clearly scoped, repeatable tasks.

Risks & Mitigation

Risk
Likelihood
Impact
Mitigation
AI Misclassification
ARIA routes a request to the wrong analyst profile, causing delay and rework — the exact problem it is meant to solve.
Medium
Medium

Classification rationale is always displayed alongside the routing decision. Analysts can re-route with one click. Monthly accuracy review refines the system prompt over time. Human override is always available — ARIA recommends, humans decide on priority.

API Key Compromise
The Anthropic API key is exposed, leading to unauthorised usage and cost liability.
Low
High

Key stored exclusively in server-side .env file, never in frontend code or browser. In production, key migrated to Azure Key Vault. Usage monitoring and spend alerts configured on Anthropic dashboard. Key rotation policy: quarterly or on any suspected exposure.

Staff Adoption Resistance
CRS staff continue submitting requests through informal channels, bypassing ARIA and rendering the system unused.
Medium
High

Adoption strategy: (1) Analysts redirect informal requests to ARIA during a 4-week transition period. (2) Analytics Lead champions the tool in team communications. (3) Requester confirmation email with ticket ID creates immediate tangible value that builds habit. (4) Leadership-visible SharePoint dashboard shows demand patterns, creating executive pull.

Data Privacy / GDPR
Request descriptions may contain personal data about CRS staff or beneficiaries that is transmitted to a third-party AI provider.
Medium
High

CRS staff are guided not to include PII or beneficiary data in request descriptions — only the nature of the analysis needed. A data handling notice is displayed on the ARIA portal. Anthropic's data processing agreement (DPA) is reviewed for compliance with GDPR Articles 5 and 28. Sensitive request channels are handled outside ARIA.

Backend Server Downtime
The Node.js server goes offline, blocking all requests from the web portal and causing the Power Automate flow to fail.
Low
Medium

For the demo: server runs locally with manual restart. For production: deploy to Azure App Service with auto-restart and health check endpoint (GET /health). Power Automate flow configured with retry logic (3 attempts, 5-minute intervals). Email submissions queue in the mailbox and are processed when server recovers.

Over-Automation Perception
Staff perceive ARIA as replacing analysts rather than supporting them, creating cultural resistance or anxiety.
Low
Medium

ARIA is framed as a triage assistant, not a decision-maker. It never rejects requests, assigns priority, or estimates cost — those remain human judgements. Communication strategy emphasises "ARIA gives your analysts more time to do the analytical work you actually need." Analysts are positioned as the intelligence layer that ARIA serves.

Prompt Injection via Email
A malicious actor sends an email containing prompt injection instructions designed to manipulate ARIA's output.
Low
Medium

The system prompt is authoritative and CRS-specific — it defines ARIA's behaviour regardless of user content. Email body is passed as a user message with clear role separation. Output is validated as structured JSON before being passed to downstream systems. Anomalous outputs are flagged manually during the monthly accuracy review.

Future Roadmap

The MVP establishes ARIA as a functioning system. The following phases outline a realistic evolution path toward a fully integrated analytics operations platform.

Phase 0 · Complete
MVP — Conversational Intake + Ticket Dashboard
Chat-first triage interface (Intake → Analysis → Refine → Ticket), multi-turn conversational refinement, structured ticket generation, live ticket history dashboard with stats and filters, four-output automation pipeline (Teams, analyst email, SharePoint, requester confirmation), backend security, rate limiting, file-based ticket persistence, and Vercel deployment support.
Phase 1 · Q3 2026
Production Deployment & Status Tracking
Deploy backend to Vercel (or Azure App Service). Set up dedicated shared mailbox and live Power Automate flow. Add ticket status management — analysts update status via Teams adaptive card; requesters can query status by replying to their confirmation email. SLA monitoring flags tickets approaching deadline. Dashboard promoted to leadership as the primary demand-visibility tool.
Phase 2 · Q4 2026
Status Tracking & Requester Self-Service
Requesters can email analytics-status@crs.org with a ticket ID to get an automated status update. Analysts can update ticket status via a Teams adaptive card. A basic SLA monitoring system flags tickets approaching deadline.
Phase 3 · H1 2027
Capacity Intelligence
ARIA cross-references incoming request complexity against the team's current sprint load and automatically suggests realistic timelines. Integrates with Microsoft Planner or Jira to check analyst availability before routing. Begins generating weekly demand summaries for the Analytics Lead.
Phase 4 · H2 2027
Sector-Wide Open Source Release
ARIA is packaged as an open-source toolkit for the humanitarian analytics community — aligned with the "data dividend" principle from the RAPIDS Framework and the NetHope Global Summit session proposal. Any NGO with a Microsoft 365 environment and an analytics team could deploy ARIA in under one day.

Conclusion

ARIA demonstrates that meaningful AI-powered automation can be designed, built, and documented within a single week — without sacrificing architectural integrity, security standards, or responsible AI principles. It solves a real problem, in a real organisation, using real technology, and produces measurable value from day one of deployment.

The project illustrates a critical principle for AI adoption in complex organisations: start with the process, not the technology. The intelligence ARIA provides is only valuable because it is embedded in a well-designed automation pipeline that closes the loop for every stakeholder — the requester, the analyst, the team lead, and the organisation's leadership.

Core Contribution

ARIA contributes to CRS's Responsible AI for Human Dignity strategy by demonstrating that AI can take on administrative overhead in a way that is transparent, secure, human-centred, and directly linked to mission impact. Every hour of analyst capacity recovered by ARIA is an hour redirected toward the analytical work that serves CRS's 130 million beneficiaries.

As a capstone for the AI for Business Leaders programme, ARIA represents the application of all four dimensions of AI fluency — understanding AI capabilities, designing AI solutions, implementing responsibly, and leading adoption — in a single, integrated, working system.
